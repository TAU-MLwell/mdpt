Hypertension is defined based on blood pressure measurements. According to the Joint National Committee (JNC) guidelines, hypertension is defined as having a systolic blood pressure (SBP) of 140 mm Hg or higher and/or a diastolic blood pressure (DBP) of 90 mm Hg or higher. However, the 2014 Hypertension guidelines updated this definition to a lower threshold, defining hypertension as having an SBP of 130 mm Hg or higher and/or a DBP of 80 mm Hg or higher.

These definitions are used to diagnose and manage hypertension, which is a major risk factor for cardiovascular diseases. The change in guidelines reflects a growing understanding of the risks associated with lower levels of blood pressure and aims to improve the management and prevention of hypertension-related complications.
